Let it be known

The pact of dorrelia

From margrave Ilia von Baltenheim

This is the Pact of Dorrelia, written and sealed by my hand, I, Margrave Ilia von Baltenheim,
as a sacred contract of vassalage and mutual accord between the League of Noble Students and the Grand Inquisitor of Incorrect Pronunciation,
dread guardian of the Spoken Word.

My lords and comrades, we find ourselves in dire days.
The cutthroats of the South do rise in defiance,
their tongues unbridled, their grammar accursed.
Discord spreads, foul utterances echo in the halls,
and curses — spoken in haste by Lords and whispered in shrieks<>
— rend the harmony of our land.

Let it be known that the noble lords of the League

Duke-Diplomat Kourosh of Leydenia
Steward Negin de Bordeleux
Marshal Mehran van Helmia
and the Grand Inquisitor of Incorrect Pronunciation herself

do hereby swear to stand as one, in peace, in loyalty,
and in wrath against all who speak ill and write worse.

So it is declared. So shall it be done.

the obligations of the league of students:

On Taxes:

Each lord shall pay their fair share of tribute once every moon
(that is, once a month), so that the High Hall may have what it needs
— warmth, light, and red pens.

On War and Defense:

When war comes, all lords must send their men,
their strength, and their voices to defend Dorrelia
from invaders — be they of foul grammar, poor spelling,
or unclear speaking.

On Giving Lectures:

Every lord must speak before the Court of the Inquisitor
at least three times during each chapter of study.
Let their voices be heard, and may their thoughts
be mostly finished.

On Heretical Speech:

The lords shall not use cursed speech too often,
for it stains the tongue and confuses
the ears.(the persian speech)

On Dark Words:

Words of darkness shall not be spoken in sacred halls,
lest the speaker be struck by awkward silence
and teacherly glare.

On Speaking Too Long:

No lord shall speak longer than is proper.
This law is especially for Kourosh of Leydenia,
who hath forgotten what silence is.

On Council with the High Teacher:

When the High Teacher calls a council,
the lords must attend, speak wisely.

On Destroying Evil Speech:

If any demon of mispronunciation appears the lords
must defeat it swiftly, with loud correction
and possibly a dictionary.

On Fighting Amongst Themselves:

No lord may start war with another lord unless
the High Teacher allows it. If they do, the Inquisition
will come, and it will not be merciful.

On Obeying the Grand Inquisitor:

Any order given by the Grand Inquisitor
that makes sense must be obeyed. If it doesn’t make
sense, it must be obeyed anyway — but with
a raised eyebrow

On the Law of Succession:

Every lord shall obay the dorrelian succesion law,
as it's the word of wises

All lords agree to follow the rules of succession in Dorrelia,
so that power passes fairly.

The obligations of the grand inquisitor of mispronunciation:

On rule of the teacher

teacher shall rule in justice and peace,
shall be just, honest and have traits of a virtue

On the overhearing sounds

teacher shall not... show anger with "voice"
, as it darkens her soul and might
damage the vassals ears

On time of war

she shall order and rule over armies and vassals,
think wisely and command

On importance of every vassal

every respected vassal must be treated with justice
no difference among any, teacher
herself is first among equals

On ownership of high penguin

high penguin, in times
if war can help in warm terrains,
and be used by anyone in time of peace.

On the door of the hall

the door of the high hall shall be
closed when possible and opened rarely

On bordered vassals

the vassals in borders must resist and be
protected by the grand inquisitor

On the decration of mortal obligations

Let it be known:

All members of the League of Students,
including the Lords, and the Grand Inquisitor,
have read, understood, and agreed to these duties. This pact exists not only to improve our language,
but to protect the learning environment, support each other’s
growth, and make our lessons both useful and enjoyable. Let us speak with clarity.
Let us learn with purpose. Let us never fear
correcting each other — even when it hurts a littl
